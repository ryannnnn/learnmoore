Is Overconfidence a Motivated Bias?
Experimental Evidence

 Does the motivation to view oneself positively lead people to hold overly positive beliefs about themselves? Our results suggest that the relationship between motivation and excessively positive self-perceptions is neither strong nor simple, but is complex and emerges only under specific conditions.




Perceptions of Algorithms

 I examined the tension between the seemingly widespread distrust of algorithms which accompanies our increasing dependence on them. When people read about either advice from an algorithm or other humans, they do not expect advice from an algorithm to be more useful than from a human, but did rely on it more. When choosing between an algorithm or person, people both expect the algorithm to give more useful advice and choose it more often than the other person.




Good Judgment Project
Improving Geopolitical Forecasts

 The Good Judgment research team is based at the University of Pennsylvania and the University of California Berkeley.

 We are participating in the Aggregative Contingent Estimation (ACE) Program, sponsored by IARPA (the U.S. Intelligence Advanced Research Projects Activity). The ACE Program aims "to dramatically enhance the accuracy, precision, and timeliness of forecasts for a broad range of event types, through the development of advanced techniques that elicit, weight, and combine the judgments of many intelligence analysts." The project is unclassified: our results will be published in traditional scholarly and scientific journals, and will be available to the general public.




Question Wording and Accuracy:
Obtaining Accurate Predictions

 Previous studies have found that people make more accurate estimates when they assign probability to preexisting intervals than when they provide a range of probable values. Our data suggest that there are situations in which the opposite is true. When and why do ranges provide more accurate estimates than intervals? When are preexisting intervals most likely to mislead people into making inaccurate estimates?
